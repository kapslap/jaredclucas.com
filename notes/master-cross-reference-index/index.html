X. The Six Agreements and Eight Divergences (Synthesis 26 — Capstone)

Six Agreements

  1. Reality is relational, not substantial. ICT = mashal = parabole. Meaning arises from comparison.
  2. Love is known by continuity, not symmetry. Aph [9] = hypomone = meno. Asymmetric endurance.
  3. Limitation enables. Stenos gate, gan fence, kathairō pruning. Boundary as habitat.
  4. Emptiness precedes creation. Tohu vavohu, kenoo, Aph [77]. The most generative begins from the most empty.
  5. Joy and pain are complementary. Aph [107] = Heb 12:2 (cross + chara). Pressing → anointing.
  6. Healing in the between. Aph [42] = mesites, synistao, trees on the banks. Coherence IS the between.

Eight Divergences

  1. Structural vs. personal — Grammar vs. speaker. Synistao (Col 1:17) = personal coherence.
  2. Evil as failure vs. agent — Dysfunction vs. enemy (embrimaomai, Matt 13:28).
  3. Surplus exceeds repair — Five forms (elevation, emphasis, novelty, conquest, transfigured wounds).
  4. Death as feature vs. enemy — Cessation vs. "the last enemy"; body redeemed, not abandoned.
  5. Ontological dependence — "Love enables choice" (Aph [1]) vs. "without me, nothing" (John 15:5).
  6. Love initiates — Enabling (Aph [1]) vs. choosing first (John 15:16).
  7. Wound as threshold, not failure — Chabburah/chabar; miphthan produces water; typos reveals.
  8. Fire = love — ICT is impersonal comparison; consuming fire is a Person (Heb 12:29 = Song 8:6).
