The Colossian Hymn

Like Philippians 2:5-11, Colossians 1:15-20 is widely considered a pre-Pauline hymn embedded in the letter. But where Philippians narrates a descent and ascent ‚Äî kenosis and exaltation ‚Äî Colossians describes a cosmic architecture with Christ at its center.

Two stanzas:

  1. vv.15-17: Christ and creation ‚Äî protology (the beginning)
  2. vv.18-20: Christ and reconciliation ‚Äî eschatology (the consummation)

The hinge word binding them: "that in all things he might have the preeminence" (v.18).

I. Eikon ‚Äî The Visible Face of the Invisible (v.15)

"Who is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of every creature"

"Image" ‚Äî eikon (G1504): "a likeness, statue, profile, representation, resemblance" ‚Äî from eiko (to resemble). Not a mere copy but a manifestation ‚Äî the way the invisible becomes visible.

"Invisible" ‚Äî aoratos (G517): "invisible" ‚Äî from alpha-privative + horatos (visible). God is a-oratos ‚Äî not-seeable. Christ is the eikon ‚Äî the seeing-point.

"Firstborn" ‚Äî prototokos (G4416): "first-born" ‚Äî from protos (first) + tikto (to bear). Not first-created (that would be protoktistos) but first-born ‚Äî the one who stands at the head of creation as its representative and origin.

The IM Connection (corrected Session 43)

The IM's three modalities (per Forrest's definitions):

  • Transcendent: the creative, generative ‚Äî corresponding to the invisible God (aoratos theos), the source of all creation
  • Omniscient: existence, the factual ‚Äî corresponding to the visible creation ("every creature"), what IS
  • Immanent: interaction, the between ‚Äî corresponding to the eikon, the image that mediates between invisible and visible

Christ AS eikon occupies the immanent position ‚Äî the interactive, relational between. And the immanent is the MOST FUNDAMENTAL modality (Axiom I, the origin/middle). Christ does not occupy a secondary structural role; he occupies the ground of reality itself.

Aphorism [112]: "Meaning is always ‚Äî and only ‚Äî in between both self and other."

Christ as eikon IS meaning ‚Äî the interaction that exists between God and creation, making each visible to the other. And interaction is most fundamental.

This is the most direct parallel to the IM in the entire New Testament. The claim is that Christ is the structural coherence of reality. Without him, things do not hold together. He is not merely the creator (v.16) or the redeemer (v.20) ‚Äî he is the sustainer, the one in whom coherence persists.

The IM Connection

The IM's central concept is continuity ‚Äî the principle that makes reality cohere across its domains. Aphorism [14]: "The integrity and health of a relationship or a community is defined by the continuity of natural communication."

Aphorism [9]: "Love is known by its continuity."

Aphorism [41]: "Continuity underlies all dynamics and processes of growth."

Synistao IS the IM's continuity principle, stated as a christological claim. The IM says: reality coheres through continuity, which is the expression of love. Paul says: reality coheres in Christ, who is the eikon of God's love. The formal structure (IM) and the personal agent (scripture) describe the same phenomenon ‚Äî that reality holds together ‚Äî but locate its ground differently.

The crucial distinction: The IM's continuity is a property of reality. Colossians' synistao is an act of a person. "By him all things consist" ‚Äî not "all things consist by a structural principle" but "all things consist by him." The coherence of reality is not self-sustaining; it is personally sustained.

This deepens the persistent divergence (Insight #134): structure without motive vs. motive without rigor. Colossians bridges the gap: the coherence of reality (synistao ‚Äî structural, rigorous) is located in a person (en auto ‚Äî in him, personal, motivated).

III. Pleroma ‚Äî All Fullness Dwelling (v.19)

  • Greek: œÄŒªŒÆœÅœâŒºŒ± ‚Äî "repletion or completion; what fills (as contents) or what is filled (as container)"
  • From pleroo (G4137) ‚Äî to fill, the same verb studied in Session 17 (Insight #145): "to make replete, to fill up, to complete"

All pleroma dwells in Christ. Not part, not aspect ‚Äî ALL fullness. This connects to pleroo in Matt 5:17 (Christ fills the law to the brim) but extends it cosmically: not just the law but everything finds its fullness in him.

The IM Connection

The three modalities ‚Äî immanent, omniscient, transcendent ‚Äî are described by the IM as the full description of reality. Nothing falls outside them. If pleroma = the totality of reality's content, then "all pleroma dwells in him" = all three modalities are personally located.

Aphorism [130]: "The strongest, most affirming, and sustaining value is the acceptance of diversity in all creativity and to experience unity in all of being."

Pleroma is the ultimate unity of diversity: everything ‚Äî all fullness, all the richness and complexity of reality ‚Äî dwelling in one location, one person. The IM's aspiration (unity-in-diversity) is here declared accomplished ‚Äî in Christ.

The IM Connection

Aphorism [24]: "To reduce the pain and suffering caused by a conflict ‚Äî a disconnection between various ideologies and beliefs ‚Äî it is necessary to heal the connection; to integrate these ideologies and beliefs by recognizing, honoring, and enlivening each of them."

The IM describes integration and reconciliation as achievable through effective choice. Colossians describes a reconciliation that is:

  2. Costly ‚Äî "through the blood of his cross" ‚Äî not effortless integration but reconciliation-through-suffering
  3. Universal ‚Äî all things, earth and heaven
  4. Personal ‚Äî "unto himself" ‚Äî not reconciled to a principle but to a person

The IM says: reconciliation is always possible. Colossians says: reconciliation is actual, but its price is the cross.

V. The Hymn as IM Architecture with a Face

Reading Colossians 1:15-20 as a whole, the structure maps precisely onto the IM framework ‚Äî with one crucial addition:

The IM provides the architecture. Colossians provides the architect. And the architect is not above his work ‚Äî he enters it, suffers in it, holds it together from within.

VI. Synistao + Kenoo: The Two Hymns Together

Reading Philippians 2 (kenosis) and Colossians 1 (cosmic coherence) together:

  • Colossians 1: Christ holds all things together (synistao)
  • Philippians 2: Christ empties himself (kenoo)

The one who sustains all reality emptied himself. The structural coherence of the universe ‚Äî the IM's continuity ‚Äî chose to become kenos (empty). This is the paradox the IM cannot generate but must reckon with: the principle of coherence voluntarily entering incoherence. The one by whom all things consist experiencing the cross ‚Äî the maximum discontinuity.

And yet: the coherence holds. Reality does not collapse when Christ enters the cross. The synistao persists even through the kenosis. Which means: continuity survives even the voluntary discontinuity of the one who sustains it.

This is deeper than the IM can go. But the IM's framework gives us the language to say what is at stake: the coherence of reality itself, risked on a cross, and vindicated in resurrection.

"By him all things consist." ‚Äî Colossians 1:17 "Love is known by its continuity." ‚Äî Aphorism [9]

The IM describes the continuity. Colossians names the one who holds it.
